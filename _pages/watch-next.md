## What to watch next

The contest is over. The closing process has its own set of inflection points.

These are not predictions. They are the specific developments that will meaningfully alter how the closing phase of this story unfolds -- and in some cases, whether it closes at all.

---

## 1. WBD consent solicitation outcome -- deadline May 26, 2026

**What to watch**
Whether the WBD subsidiary issuers receive the required majority in aggregate principal amount of consents across all 17 note series by the deadline of 5:00 p.m. New York time on May 26, 2026.

**Why it matters**
The consent solicitations are a prerequisite to Paramount's tender and exchange offers. Without the required consents, the Paramount offers cannot proceed, and the debt management exercise that is central to financing the combined company's permanent capital structure stalls. This is not a formality -- it is a gating condition with a hard deadline.

**What it could signal**
A clean result across all series confirms institutional alignment with the transaction and clears the path to the June 17 offer expiration. A shortfall on any series would require Paramount and WBD to assess options, which could include waiving the condition on that series, extending the solicitation, or adjusting terms. Any of those scenarios introduces delay and uncertainty into the closing timeline.

---

## 2. Tender and exchange offer participation -- expiration June 17, 2026

**What to watch**
The participation rate in Paramount's cash tender offers (two short-dated series, approximately $2.44 billion aggregate principal) and exchange offers (thirteen longer-dated series, approximately $12.7 billion aggregate principal including euro-denominated notes). Both sets of offers expire at 5:00 p.m. New York time on June 17, 2026.

**Why it matters**
High participation in the exchange offers directly shapes the combined company's permanent capital structure. Notes that are not exchanged remain outstanding obligations of the WBD subsidiary issuers after closing, adding complexity to the post-close capital structure. The exchange offers are restricted to qualified institutional buyers and non-U.S. persons under Regulation S -- non-QIB domestic holders are ineligible, which sets a ceiling on participation regardless of sentiment.

**What it could signal**
Strong exchange offer participation signals institutional confidence in Paramount's credit as the combined obligor and supports the bridge refinancing plan. Lower-than-expected participation would leave more WBD subsidiary obligations outstanding post-close, potentially complicating the first-lien and second-lien refinancing that replaces the $49 billion bridge loan.

## 3. DOJ antitrust review

**What to watch**
Any public disclosures, statements, or developments related to the Department of Justice Antitrust Division's review of the transaction. The DOJ head stated publicly in March 2026 that Paramount will receive no fast-track treatment.

**Why it matters**
DOJ clearance is a required closing condition. A horizontal combination of this scale -- two major studio and network businesses -- raises substantive concentration questions that the review process will address. The nature and timing of any second request, if issued, would be the most significant near-term signal about the trajectory of the review.

**What it could signal**
Unconditional clearance would confirm Q3 2026 as a realistic close target. Clearance with conditions (structural or behavioral remedies) would require assessment of whether Paramount accepts the conditions or negotiates them. Extended review or litigation would push the close timeline beyond Q3 2026 and bring the March 4, 2027 Merger Agreement end date into sharper focus.

---

## 4. European regulatory clearance

**What to watch**
Filing and review milestones for European regulatory approval of the transaction, including any formal phase one or phase two investigations initiated by the European Commission or relevant national competition authorities.

**Why it matters**
European regulatory clearance is a separate required closing condition running on its own calendar. The combined entity's European footprint -- including WBD's free-to-air channels, streaming presence, and content distribution -- creates a reviewable footprint that may raise independent concerns from European regulators even if the DOJ process resolves smoothly.

**What it could signal**
Phase one clearance would signal that European regulators do not see material concentration concerns requiring in-depth review. A phase two investigation would extend the European timeline and could introduce remedies that complicate the combined company's European operations independently of whatever the DOJ requires.

---

## 5. California Attorney General investigation

**What to watch**
Any formal filings, public statements, or substantive developments from California Attorney General Rob Bonta's office regarding its reported investigation of the transaction.

**Why it matters**
The California AG investigation was disclosed as a risk factor in connection with the April 2026 shareholder vote. California has independent authority to review media transactions with significant in-state footprints, and the combined WBD-Paramount entity -- with major studio operations in Burbank and Los Angeles -- has a substantial California presence.

**What it could signal**
A formal challenge from the California AG could introduce a parallel legal track that complicates or delays close even if federal and European reviews proceed smoothly. Alternatively, the investigation could resolve without formal action. The absence of public developments on this front is not the same as resolution.

---

## 6. Acquisition closing and settlement of debt offers

**What to watch**
The actual closing date of the acquisition and the concurrent settlement of the Paramount tender and exchange offers, both of which are expected to occur on or shortly after the closing date.

**Why it matters**
Closing is conditioned on all regulatory approvals being in place. Settlement of the debt offers is conditioned on closing. The entire debt management exercise -- consents, tender, exchange -- resolves at the same moment the acquisition closes. That creates a concentrated operational event: simultaneous equity acquisition, debt instrument settlement across multiple series, and obligor substitution on the exchange offer notes, all on a single settlement date.

**What it could signal**
A Q3 2026 close confirms the transaction timeline and activates the combined company's operating structure. A delay into Q4 2026 or beyond would extend the period during which WBD operates as a standalone company with its existing capital structure, and would bring the March 4, 2027 Merger Agreement end date into view as a risk factor.

---

## 7. Bridge loan refinancing

**What to watch**
Paramount's execution of the permanent debt financing that replaces its $49 billion acquisition bridge loan -- approximately $39.5 billion of first-lien secured debt and $12.4 billion of second-lien secured debt, subject to market conditions.

**Why it matters**
The bridge loan is a short-term financing instrument. The permanent capital structure must be put in place around or after closing. Market conditions, credit spreads, and ratings agency assessments at the time of execution will determine the actual cost of that financing, which in turn affects the combined company's free cash flow and its ability to meet the deleveraging commitments made to ratings agencies (3.75x net debt to EBITDA by 2028, 3.0x by 2029).

**What it could signal**
Smooth refinancing at competitive rates signals market confidence in the combined entity's credit profile and synergy delivery prospects. Difficult market conditions or ratings agency concerns at the time of refinancing could raise the cost of capital and pressure the deleveraging timeline, which would attract attention in every subsequent earnings call and credit review.

---

## 8. Synergy delivery against disclosed targets

**What to watch**
Post-close disclosures and earnings communications related to cost and revenue synergy realization against Paramount's disclosed target of over $6 billion in combined synergies, with approximately 30% expected in year one, 70% by year two, and full run-rate by year three.

**Why it matters**
The synergy targets were disclosed to eligible noteholders as part of the exchange offer materials. They form the revenue and cost side of the deleveraging commitment. If year-one realization falls materially short of the 30% target, the combined company faces pressure on multiple fronts simultaneously: higher leverage, lower cash flow, and reduced credibility in ratings agency conversations.

**What it could signal**
Early synergy delivery above target would support the deleveraging commitments and reduce refinancing risk. A shortfall would raise questions about the integration plan, management execution, and whether the combined company's capital structure is sustainable at the leverage levels incurred to complete the acquisition.

## Final note

The bidding war generated daily headlines. The closing phase generates fewer of them, but the developments that matter are just as consequential.

Regulatory decisions, debt management outcomes, and operational execution at closing will determine whether the value created by the transaction is actually delivered. This page will be updated as those signals emerge.
