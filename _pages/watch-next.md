*Updated May 2026. All prior watch items related to the bidding contest, proxy campaign, and Delaware litigation are resolved. Items below reflect the closing phase.*

---

The contest ended February 27, 2026. The closing process has its own inflection points.

These are the specific developments that will alter how the closing phase unfolds, and in some cases whether it closes at all.

---

## 1. WBD consent solicitation outcome

**Deadline: May 26, 2026 at 5:00 p.m. New York time**

Whether the WBD subsidiary issuers receive required majority consents across all 17 note series. Missing the threshold on any series would require Paramount and WBD to assess options including waiving the condition, extending the solicitation, or adjusting terms. Any of those scenarios introduces delay.

---

## 2. Paramount tender and exchange offer participation

**Expiration: June 17, 2026 at 5:00 p.m. New York time**

Participation rates in the cash tender offers (two short-dated series, approximately $2.44 billion aggregate) and exchange offers (thirteen longer-dated series, approximately $12.7 billion aggregate including euro-denominated notes). Notes not exchanged remain outstanding WBD subsidiary obligations after close. The QIB and Regulation S eligibility restriction on exchange offers sets a ceiling on participation that is independent of holder intent.

---

## 3. DOJ antitrust review

The DOJ head stated publicly in March 2026 that Paramount will not receive fast-track treatment. The substantive question is what theory of harm drives the review and what conditions, if any, the DOJ attaches to clearance. Structural remedies would affect the combined company's asset profile and disclosed synergy targets. Extended second-request review would push the close timeline beyond Q3 2026 and bring the March 4, 2027 Merger Agreement end date into focus.

---

## 4. European regulatory clearance

A separate required closing condition running on its own calendar. Phase one clearance signals no material concentration concerns requiring in-depth review. A phase two investigation would extend the European timeline independently of whatever the DOJ requires and could introduce conditions affecting the combined company's European operations.

---

## 5. California Attorney General investigation

Disclosed as a risk factor in connection with the April 2026 shareholder vote. A formal challenge from the California AG would introduce a parallel legal track. No public resolution has been announced. Absence of news is not resolution.

---

## 6. Acquisition closing and debt offer settlement

Closing is conditioned on all regulatory approvals. Settlement of the tender and exchange offers occurs on or shortly after close. Both events land on the same date, creating a concentrated operational moment: equity acquisition, debt instrument settlement across multiple series, and obligor substitution on exchange notes, all simultaneously.

---

## 7. Bridge loan refinancing

Paramount needs to replace its $49 billion bridge loan with approximately $39.5 billion of first-lien and $12.4 billion of second-lien secured debt. Market conditions at the time of execution will determine the actual cost, which feeds directly into the combined company's ability to meet its deleveraging commitments to ratings agencies: 3.75x by 2028, 3.0x by 2029.

---

## 8. Year-one synergy delivery

Paramount disclosed a target of over $6 billion in total synergies with approximately 30% in year one. If early delivery falls short, the combined company faces simultaneous pressure on leverage, cash flow, and ratings agency credibility. That is the scenario worth watching once the transaction closes.

---

The closing phase generates fewer headlines than the bidding war. The developments listed here are no less consequential.
