## Active

### Regulatory

**1. Will the DOJ approve the transaction, and on what timeline?**
The DOJ head stated in March 2026 that Paramount will receive no fast-track treatment. What theory of harm drives the review? Does it conclude before the March 4, 2027 Merger Agreement end date?

**2. What conditions, if any, does the DOJ attach to clearance?**
Structural remedies (asset divestitures) would change the combined company's operating profile and could affect disclosed synergy targets. Behavioral remedies (conduct commitments) could constrain commercial flexibility for years after close.

**3. What is the European regulatory timeline?**
Does the combined entity's European footprint raise concentration concerns requiring phase two review? Does the European process run parallel to DOJ, or does one conclude first?

**4. What does the California AG investigation produce?**
A formal legal challenge would introduce a parallel track even if federal and European reviews proceed cleanly. No public resolution has been announced.

**5. Does regulatory review push the close beyond Q3 2026?**
How close is the Q3 2026 target to the actual regulatory calendar? What triggers the March 4, 2027 end date as a live risk?

---

### Debt management operations

**6. Do the WBD consent solicitations achieve required majorities across all 17 series?**
Are there individual series where holder concentration or mandate constraints create uncertainty about hitting the required threshold by May 26, 2026?

**7. What is the exchange offer participation rate?**
Notes not exchanged remain outstanding WBD subsidiary obligations after close. The QIB eligibility restriction sets a ceiling on participation independent of holder sentiment. How much of the approximately $12.7 billion exchange offer pool actually exchanges?

**8. If the acquisition does not close, what are the fallback mechanics?**
The consent amendments and Paramount offers are all conditioned on close. If the Merger Agreement is terminated, the WBD indenture amendments specify fallback terms, and the Paramount offers terminate without settlement. Have holders reviewed those provisions?

**9. How does the temporary identifier mechanic affect secondary market liquidity?**
Consenting holders receive a temporary CUSIP or ISIN, creating two trading populations per series between consent delivery and offer expiration. Does that split create bid-ask disruption or position management complications?

---

### Post-close

**10. How does the permanent debt financing execute?**
The $49 billion bridge loan needs to be replaced. Market conditions at execution determine actual borrowing costs. Does Paramount have flexibility on timing or structure if conditions are unfavorable at close?

**11. Are the deleveraging commitments achievable?**
Below 3.75x net debt to EBITDA by 2028, 3.0x by 2029. What are the realistic downside scenarios, and how much headroom does the plan carry?

**12. Does year-one synergy delivery reach the 30% target?**
Over $6 billion total, approximately 30% in year one. Integration plans of this complexity rarely execute exactly as modeled. Where is the plan most exposed?

**13. What does the combined content strategy look like?**
Two studios, two streaming platforms, and legacy cable networks create decisions that go beyond cost cutting. How does the combined entity manage IP and distribution without cannibalizing its own assets?

---

### The underlying questions

**14. At what price does regulatory risk become a deal risk?**
The $5.8 billion reverse termination fee defines the financial stakes of a regulatory failure. The reputational and strategic costs of a protracted battle extend beyond that number. How does each party's view of those broader costs shape how they negotiate with regulators over remedies?

**15. Does the combined company's strategic thesis prove out?**
Scale in content, streaming, and distribution requires execution in a market still in transition. The thesis will be judged over years, not quarters. Whether the combination delivers on it is the question that defines the deal's legacy.

## Resolved (archived)

**Board process and fiduciary posture:** resolved February 26-27, 2026. The WBD board declared Paramount's $31.00 offer a Superior Proposal after receiving a Netflix waiver. Netflix declined to match. WBD signed with Paramount.

**Netflix vs. Paramount transaction architecture:** resolved. The Netflix agreement was terminated February 27, 2026. Discovery Global separation is no longer required.

**Shareholder vote:** resolved April 23, 2026. Over 1.7 billion in favor against approximately 16.3 million against.

**Delaware Chancery Court litigation:** resolved. Paramount's disclosure lawsuit was superseded by the Superior Proposal determination and the execution of the definitive agreement.

**Proxy contest:** resolved. Paramount's director nomination campaign was withdrawn following the definitive agreement.

**Netflix all-cash amendment:** historical. Netflix converted to all-cash on January 20, 2026, and withdrew February 27, 2026.
