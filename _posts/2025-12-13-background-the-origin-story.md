## What changed

- This post establishes the transaction background that created Warner Bros. Discovery and shaped the capital structure that still matters for today’s contested situation.

## Key facts

- On May 17, 2021, AT&T announced a transaction to separate WarnerMedia and combine it with Discovery.
- The transaction closed on April 11, 2022, creating Warner Bros. Discovery as a publicly traded company.

## Banker commentary

This is the prologue to the story that follows. The AT&T and Discovery transaction created a scaled content platform, but it also set the initial contours of strategic flexibility. For executives tracking today’s contested dynamics, it is difficult to separate the current decision set from the capital structure and operating realities created at inception.
