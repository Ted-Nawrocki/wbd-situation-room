With the shareholder vote behind them and regulatory approval ahead, Paramount Skydance and Warner Bros. Discovery launched three concurrent debt management operations on May 19, 2026. All three are linked. All three are conditional on the acquisition closing. Two of the three have deadlines measured in days.

## Just the facts

**WBD consent solicitations:**
- Issuers: Discovery Global Holdings, Inc. (DGH) and Discovery Communications, LLC (DCL)
- Scope: 17 series of outstanding notes; interest rates 3.950% to 5.300%; maturities 2027 to 2052
- Purpose: extend the deadline for Required Exchange Transactions from December 30, 2026 to March 4, 2027; additional amendments address Junior Lien Exchange Note terms in both close and no-close scenarios
- Consent threshold: majority in aggregate principal amount per indenture
- Consent deadline: May 26, 2026 at 5:00 p.m. New York time
- Consent payment: $2.50 per $1,000 principal amount (EUR 2.50 per EUR 1,000), funded by Paramount
- Payment expected: on or about May 29, 2026
- Solicitation agents: BofA Securities and Citigroup
- Tabulation and information agent: Global Bondholder Services Corporation

**Paramount cash tender offers:**
- Target series (two only, both short-dated):
  - 3.755% Senior Notes due 2027 (DGH Issuer): $1,195,271,000 outstanding
  - 3.950% Senior Notes due 2028 (DCL Issuer): $1,249,026,000 outstanding
- Consideration: cash at a fixed spread of 0 basis points over the applicable U.S. Treasury benchmark, set at 10:00 a.m. New York time on the expiration date, plus accrued interest
- Structure: any-and-all; no proration; cap of $2.4 billion reflects total outstanding of the two series
- Expiration: June 17, 2026 at 5:00 p.m. New York time
- Settlement: on or shortly after acquisition closing, targeted Q3 2026
- Accepted notes: cancelled and retired at settlement

**Paramount exchange offers:**
- Target series: thirteen longer-dated series, maturities 2029 to 2052, USD and euro denominated, from both DGH and DCL issuers
- Aggregate outstanding: approximately $12.1 billion USD plus approximately EUR 574 million
- Consideration: newly issued Paramount Skydance second-lien secured notes with matching currency, maturity, interest payment dates, and rates
- Exchange ratio: $1,000 (or EUR 1,000) principal for $1,000 (or EUR 1,000) principal; no cash premium
- Structure: any-and-all; no proration; cap of $12.8 billion reflects total outstanding of the thirteen series
- Expiration: June 17, 2026 at 5:00 p.m. New York time
- Settlement: on or shortly after acquisition closing, targeted Q3 2026
- Eligibility: qualified institutional buyers under Rule 144A or non-U.S. persons under Regulation S only; non-QIB domestic holders are not eligible

**Conditions applying to both offers:**
- Requisite consents received in the WBD consent solicitations
- Consummation of the acquisition
- Participation requires prior consent delivery; consenting holders receive a temporary CUSIP or ISIN and only notes bearing that identifier are eligible to participate

**Agents:** BofA Securities and Citigroup (dealer managers); Global Bondholder Services Corporation (exchange agent and information agent); offer documents at gbsc-usa.com/paramount

## Broader financing context

Paramount plans to replace its $49 billion bridge loan with approximately $39.5 billion of first-lien secured debt and $12.4 billion of second-lien secured debt. The new PSKY exchange notes form part of the second-lien layer. Paramount has committed to ratings agencies to reach below 3.75x net debt to adjusted EBITDA by 2028 and 3.0x by 2029. Disclosed synergy target: over $6 billion in combined cost and revenue synergies, with approximately 30% in year one, 70% by year two, full run-rate by year three.

## Ted's Takes

The consent solicitation is a prerequisite, not a standalone event. Without required majorities across each indenture, the Paramount offers cannot proceed. The proposed amendments are scheduling adjustments only; no economic terms change. The $2.50 fee compensates holders for their time, not a concession. The five-day window from May 19 to May 26 is short by design and demands prompt internal escalation from any holder who needs to review and instruct.

The split between cash tender and exchange reflects basic maturity economics. The 2027 and 2028 notes mature in 12 to 24 months. Replacing them with long-dated Paramount secured notes would create a mismatch that serves no one. Cash retirement is the right answer. The thirteen longer-dated series have enough remaining life to make the obligor substitution rational. But holders exchanging into new PSKY notes are not simply reboooking a position. The obligor changes from a WBD subsidiary to Paramount Skydance. The security package moves from unsecured to second-lien. The combined entity carries considerably more leverage than the WBD subsidiary obligors carry today. The coupon is the same. The credit is not.

Three processing details that require attention before June 17. First, exchange offer eligibility is restricted to QIBs and Regulation S holders; the screen must be run before submitting instructions, not after. Second, consenting holders receive a temporary CUSIP or ISIN; those notes trade separately from non-consenting positions in the same series, creating two populations that must be tracked simultaneously. Third, settlement does not occur at expiration. It occurs on or shortly after acquisition close, leaving positions in a tendered-but-unsettled state for a window that could extend months depending on the DOJ and European regulatory calendars.
