The largest remaining hurdle moved today. On June 12, 2026, the U.S. Department of Justice Antitrust Division cleared Paramount Skydance's acquisition of Warner Bros. Discovery without conditions, according to multiple reports. The clearance carries no divestitures, no behavioral remedies, and no concessions.

## Just the facts

- Politico first reported the clearance. Variety and NBC News corroborated, each citing people familiar with the matter.
- DOJ officials concluded the transaction did not pose a threat to competition and declined to challenge it.
- The clearance carries no divestitures, behavioral remedies, or other concessions.
- Paramount issued a statement thanking the Department for its review and noting that other agencies had completed reviews and provided clearance to date.
- As of the initial reporting, no formal DOJ press release had posted, and no confirmed closing date had been disclosed.
- The California Attorney General's office, joined by the New York Attorney General's office, continues an active review.
- PSKY shares rose approximately 3.8% and WBD approximately 0.4% in Friday post-close trading.

## Where this leaves the deal

Three gating items stood between the signed agreement and closing: shareholder approval, DOJ clearance, and the broader set of regulatory reviews. Shareholders approved on April 23. The DOJ has now cleared. Paramount has indicated other agencies that reviewed the deal have provided clearance to date, and German foreign investment authorities cleared the tender offer back in January.

What remains is the state attorney general review. California's office, joined by New York's, continues to look at the transaction. A state review does not halt a closing on its own, but a coordinated action could introduce a parallel track. Until that resolves, the closing timeline stays undisclosed.

## Ted's Takes

Unconditional is the word that matters. A clearance that required divestitures would have forced a reshuffle of the asset perimeter Paramount agreed to buy, which would have rippled into the synergy targets disclosed to noteholders and the leverage math behind the financing. Clearing the deal as filed keeps all of that intact.

For those of us processing the debt side, this tightens the link between the offers and the close. The tender and exchange offers settle on the acquisition closing, and the closing's principal federal obstacle is now behind it. The remaining question is the state attorneys general. I am treating the reporting as strong but not yet official, since the DOJ statement had not posted at the time of the first reports. The terms are worth confirming against the Department's own release once it appears.
