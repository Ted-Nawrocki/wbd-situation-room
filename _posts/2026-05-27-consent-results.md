The consent leg is done. On May 27, 2026, Paramount Skydance announced that the WBD subsidiary issuers received the requisite consents across all three existing WBD indentures. The solicitations expired May 26, and supplemental indentures were executed the same day.

## Just the facts

- Issuers: Discovery Global Holdings, Inc. and Discovery Communications, LLC
- Requisite consents received on all three existing WBD indentures
- Solicitations expired at 5:00 p.m. New York time on May 26, 2026
- Supplemental indentures executed May 26, 2026
- The supplemental indentures became effective on execution but become operative only on the consent payment date, expected on or about May 29, 2026
- Paramount funded the consent payment using cash on hand, on the issuers' behalf, and is obligated to fund it whether or not the acquisition closes
- The tender and exchange offers expire June 17, 2026, subject to extension; settlement is conditioned on the closing of the acquisition
- Paramount stated it currently anticipates extending the offer expiration date until the time of consummation of the acquisition

## Ted's Takes

A consent solicitation that clears on the first pass is a quiet event, and that is the point. The requisite consents across all three indentures remove the condition that sat in front of Paramount's offers. The line in the release that custody teams should mark is the extension intent. Paramount plans to extend the June 17 expiration to track the closing rather than letting the offers lapse and relaunching them. That keeps tendered positions live and preserves withdrawal rights through the expiration as extended, which is a cleaner path than a stop-start sequence would have been.
