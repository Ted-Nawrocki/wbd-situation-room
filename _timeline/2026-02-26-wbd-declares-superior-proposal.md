title: "WBD Board Determines Paramount's Revised $31.00 Offer Is a Superior Proposal"
fact: "On February 26, 2026, after receiving a contractual waiver from Netflix permitting engagement with Paramount, WBD's board of directors reopened negotiations with Paramount Skydance. The board unanimously determined that Paramount's revised all-cash offer of $31.00 per share, valuing the entire WBD enterprise at approximately $110.9 billion, constituted a 'Superior Proposal' under the terms of its existing merger agreement with Netflix. The $31.00 price represents a $3.25 premium over Netflix's $27.75 per share offer. The Superior Proposal determination triggered a contractual notice period during which Netflix had the right to revise its offer to match or exceed Paramount's terms. Paramount's revised offer covered the entire WBD enterprise and did not require completion of the Discovery Global separation."
context: "The Netflix waiver was not a formality. Under the Netflix merger agreement, WBD was prohibited from engaging with competing bidders without Netflix's consent. Netflix had to actively grant permission. That decision reflects Netflix's own analysis: at $31.00 for the whole enterprise, matching would have required Netflix to assign value to the Global Networks business it had no use for. Granting the waiver and collecting a $2.8 billion termination fee was the rational outcome. The Superior Proposal determination is a defined contractual term. By making it, the board formally triggered Netflix's matching rights. Everything that followed turned on whether Netflix chose to match."
