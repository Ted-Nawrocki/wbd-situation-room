title: "Netflix Declines to Match; WBD Signs Definitive Agreement with Paramount at $31.00 per Share"
fact: "On February 27, 2026, Netflix co-CEOs Ted Sarandos and Greg Peters announced that Netflix would not raise its offer, declining to match Paramount Skydance's $31.00 per share bid. They stated they were 'declining to match' after determining the deal was no longer financially attractive at that price. With Netflix's matching rights expired, WBD and Paramount Skydance signed a definitive Agreement and Plan of Merger. Paramount agreed to acquire all outstanding WBD shares at $31.00 per share in cash, representing a total enterprise value of approximately $110.9 billion, covering the entire WBD enterprise with no separation required. The Merger Agreement sets a deal end date of March 4, 2027, subject to extension. Paramount committed to a reverse termination fee of $5.8 billion if the transaction fails to close for regulatory reasons. WBD's termination of its Netflix merger agreement triggered the $2.8 billion termination fee payable to Netflix, funded by Paramount."
context: "Netflix's phrase 'declining to match' is precise. At $31.00 for the whole company, the deal no longer worked for Netflix shareholders. Matching would have meant paying for Global Networks, a business Netflix had no use for. The co-CEOs framed the exit as a financial decision, not a concession, which was the correct posture. For WBD shareholders, the outcome was materially better than December. The original Netflix transaction implied $27.75 plus Discovery Global shares through a multi-step process. The Paramount transaction delivers $31.00 in cash, no separation required. Paramount absorbed the $2.8 billion Netflix termination fee as the known cost of winning."
