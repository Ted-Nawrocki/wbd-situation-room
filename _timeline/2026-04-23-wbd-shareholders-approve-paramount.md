title: "WBD Shareholders Overwhelmingly Approve Paramount Skydance Merger"
fact: "On April 23, 2026, Warner Bros. Discovery stockholders voted at a Special Meeting to approve the previously announced transaction with Paramount Skydance Corporation. The company secretary announced the preliminary results, characterizing the vote as 'overwhelming': over 1.7 billion votes were cast in favor of the Paramount merger, compared to approximately 16.3 million votes against. Shareholders did not approve the executive compensation package presented at the same meeting. The transaction remains subject to regulatory clearances, including review by the U.S. Department of Justice Antitrust Division and European regulators. Both companies continue to target closing in the third quarter of 2026. In connection with the shareholder vote, Paramount noted it had successfully completed equity and debt syndications and reported progress across regulatory approvals."
context: "A vote of this magnitude -- 1.7 billion for against 16.3 million against -- eliminates shareholder opposition as a meaningful risk factor. With equity financing complete, debt syndications closed, and shareholders onside, the remaining gating items are regulatory. That is a meaningfully different risk profile than the situation three months ago, when the outcome was genuinely uncertain.\n\nThe failed say-on-pay vote is worth noting even though it carries no binding effect. Advisory votes on executive compensation can serve as a signal from institutional shareholders about governance concerns. In a transaction context, they rarely derail deals, but they can generate proxy advisor and press attention that management prefers to avoid. Given the scale of the pro-merger vote, it is clear that the compensation objection did not represent a broader protest vote against the transaction itself.\n\nFor corporate actions practitioners, the shareholder approval milestone marks the transition from a contested, multi-party situation to a single-track regulatory waiting game. The processing implications shift accordingly. The open items now are: DOJ review, European regulatory clearances, and the concurrent debt management exercise -- the consent solicitations and tender and exchange offers launched separately on May 19, 2026. Each of those has its own deadline, its own eligibility structure, and its own settlement mechanics. The administrative load remains substantial even after the shareholder vote removes the equity side uncertainty."
