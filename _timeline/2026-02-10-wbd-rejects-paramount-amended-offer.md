title: "WBD Board Unanimously Rejects Paramount's Revised Offer"
fact: "On February 10, 2026, Paramount Skydance filed an 8-K announcing it had submitted a revised offer letter to the WBD board and amended and extended its tender offer for all outstanding WBD shares. WBD's board, through Chairman Samuel A. Di Piazza Jr., issued a unanimous rejection. Di Piazza stated the board had 'unanimously determined that Paramount's latest offer remains inferior to our merger agreement with Netflix across multiple key areas.' In a letter to shareholders, he wrote that Paramount's offer continues to provide 'insufficient value' and carries 'significant costs, risks and uncertainties as compared to the Netflix merger,' specifically citing the risk that a failed Paramount closing would result in 'potentially considerable value destruction.' WBD CEO David Zaslav told employees in an internal memo that the company's 'operating plans remain unchanged' and its priorities for 2026 are 'clear and intentional.'"
context: "The board shifted its language from December. Earlier rejections focused on process. This one attacked Paramount's execution risk directly, arguing the structure created 'potentially considerable value destruction' in a failure scenario. That is a specific fiduciary finding Paramount would need to rebut, not just outbid. Each tender extension also required new Schedule TO amendments and updated holder communications. The accumulated weight of multiple extensions without board support raised a straightforward question about how long the offer retained credibility."
