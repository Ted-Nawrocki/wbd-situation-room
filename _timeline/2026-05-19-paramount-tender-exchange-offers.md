title: "Paramount Launches $2.4 Billion Cash Tender Offers and $12.8 Billion Exchange Offers on WBD Notes"
fact: "On May 19, 2026, Paramount Skydance Corporation announced the commencement of concurrent but separate cash tender offers and exchange offers targeting notes issued by Discovery Global Holdings, Inc. (DGH Issuer) and Discovery Communications, LLC (DCL Issuer).\n\nThe tender offers target two short-dated series only: the 3.755% Senior Notes due 2027 (DGH Issuer, $1,195,271,000 outstanding) and the 3.950% Senior Notes due 2028 (DCL Issuer, $1,249,026,000 outstanding). Aggregate outstanding principal of the two tender offer series is approximately $2.44 billion. Holders whose notes are accepted will receive cash consideration calculated by reference to a fixed spread of 0 basis points over the applicable U.S. Treasury benchmark, determined at 10:00 a.m. New York time on the expiration date. Accepted notes will be cancelled and retired.\n\nThe exchange offers target thirteen longer-dated series ranging in maturity from 2029 to 2052, across both USD and euro denominations, from both issuers. Aggregate outstanding principal of the exchange offer series is approximately $12.1 billion USD-denominated plus approximately EUR 574 million euro-denominated. Eligible holders who validly tender will receive newly issued Paramount Skydance second-lien secured notes with matching currency, maturity dates, interest payment dates, and interest rates, with certain exceptions as specified in the offering memorandum. No cash payment or other premium is offered in the exchange offers.\n\nBoth sets of offers are structured as any-and-all offers with no proration mechanism. Each series is a separate offer and may be individually consummated, amended, extended, terminated, or withdrawn without affecting any other series. Both sets of offers expire at 5:00 p.m. New York time on June 17, 2026. Settlement is expected on or shortly after the acquisition closing date, targeted Q3 2026.\n\nBoth offers are conditioned on receipt of requisite noteholder consents in the concurrent WBD consent solicitations and on consummation of the acquisition. Participation in either offer requires prior delivery of consents. Upon delivering consents, holders receive a temporary identifier (temporary CUSIP or ISIN) for their applicable notes; only notes bearing a temporary identifier are eligible to participate in the offers.\n\nThe exchange offers are restricted to eligible holders only: holders must be either qualified institutional buyers as defined under Rule 144A of the Securities Act of 1933, or non-U.S. persons as defined under Regulation S. Non-QIB domestic holders are not eligible to participate in the exchange offers.\n\nParamount has disclosed plans to replace its $49 billion acquisition bridge loan with approximately $39.5 billion of first-lien secured debt and $12.4 billion of second-lien secured debt, subject to market conditions. Paramount has committed to ratings agencies to delever to below 3.75x net debt to adjusted EBITDA by 2028 and 3.0x by 2029. Paramount also disclosed to eligible holders a target of over $6 billion in combined cost and revenue synergies, with approximately 30% expected within the first year post-close, 70% by year two, and full run-rate by year three.\n\nBofA Securities and Citigroup are acting as dealer managers. Global Bondholder Services Corporation is acting as exchange agent and information agent. The eligibility certification and offer documents are available at gbsc-usa.com/paramount."
context: "The structural split between the tender and exchange offers is deliberate and worth understanding precisely.\n\nThe two short-dated notes -- the 2027 and 2028 maturities -- are being taken out for cash. They mature so soon that substituting them with newly issued long-dated Paramount secured notes would create a maturity mismatch that makes no economic sense for either side. Cash retirement is the clean solution: Paramount pays par plus accrued interest, the instruments are cancelled, and the WBD issuers' obligations on those two series are extinguished at closing.\n\nThe thirteen longer-dated notes -- 2029 through 2052 -- are being exchanged for newly issued Paramount second-lien secured notes with matching terms. These instruments have enough remaining life to make the obligor substitution commercially rational. From the combined company's perspective, replacing WBD subsidiary obligations with Paramount parent obligations on the same economic terms integrates the capital structure without forcing an immediate cash outlay on instruments that have years of runway. From a holder's perspective, the exchange is a credit substitution: the obligor changes from a WBD subsidiary to Paramount Skydance, and the security package changes from unsecured to second-lien secured. The coupon and maturity are preserved, but the credit exposure is now to a substantially more leveraged combined entity.\n\nThe any-and-all structure eliminates proration risk. The caps -- $2.4 billion for the tender, $12.8 billion for the exchange -- are sized by reference to the total outstanding principal of each respective pool, not set below it. Under full participation, both pools are accommodated within the stated caps.\n\nThe eligibility restriction on the exchange offers is operationally significant. Only QIBs and non-U.S. persons under Regulation S may participate. Custodians and intermediaries must screen their holder populations and ensure only eligible holders submit exchange instructions. Non-eligible holders of exchange offer notes who have already delivered consents will receive the consent payment, but cannot receive new PSKY notes. Their existing notes will remain outstanding obligations of the WBD issuer subsidiaries until maturity or other disposition.\n\nThe temporary identifier mechanic deserves attention. Once a holder delivers consent, their notes are re-identified with a temporary CUSIP or ISIN and begin trading separately from non-consenting holders' notes. This creates two distinct trading populations for each series during the window between consent delivery and offer expiration. Systems that do not handle temporary identifiers cleanly will create reconciliation problems. Operations teams should verify their platforms can track both the original and temporary identifiers simultaneously and link them correctly at settlement.\n\nFinally, settlement does not occur at expiration. Both offers settle on or shortly after the acquisition closing date, which is targeted for Q3 2026 but subject to DOJ and European regulatory timelines. A holder who tenders on June 16 may wait months for settlement. During that window, tendered notes remain outstanding obligations of the WBD issuers, the obligor has not yet changed, and positions need to be tracked in a tendered-but-unsettled state. That is a non-trivial custody management challenge across what could be a multi-month window."
