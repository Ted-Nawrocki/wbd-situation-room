title: "Paramount Launches $2.4 Billion Cash Tender Offers and $12.8 Billion Exchange Offers on WBD Notes"
fact: "On May 19, 2026, Paramount Skydance Corporation announced concurrent but separate cash tender offers and exchange offers on notes issued by Discovery Global Holdings, Inc. and Discovery Communications, LLC. The tender offers and exchange offers target entirely different series. The tender offers cover two short-dated series: the 3.755% Senior Notes due 2027 (DGH Issuer, $1,195,271,000 outstanding) and the 3.950% Senior Notes due 2028 (DCL Issuer, $1,249,026,000 outstanding), capped at $2.4 billion in aggregate. Holders whose notes are accepted receive cash and have their instruments cancelled and retired. The exchange offers cover thirteen longer-dated series from 2029 to 2052 across USD and euro denominations from both issuers, capped at $12.8 billion. Holders who exchange receive newly issued Paramount Skydance second-lien secured notes with matching currency, maturity dates, interest payment dates, and interest rates, with certain exceptions. Both sets of offers are any-and-all with no proration mechanism. Each series is a separate offer. Both expire at 5:00 p.m. New York time on June 17, 2026. Settlement is expected on or shortly after the acquisition closing date, targeted Q3 2026. The exchange offers are restricted to qualified institutional buyers under Rule 144A or non-U.S. persons under Regulation S. Non-QIB domestic holders are not eligible. Participation in either offer requires prior delivery of consents in the concurrent WBD consent solicitations. Upon delivering consent, holders receive a temporary CUSIP or ISIN; only notes bearing a temporary identifier are eligible to participate. Paramount plans to replace its $49 billion bridge loan with approximately $39.5 billion of first-lien and $12.4 billion of second-lien secured debt. Paramount has committed to ratings agencies to delever to below 3.75x net debt to adjusted EBITDA by 2028 and 3.0x by 2029. Paramount has also disclosed a target of over $6 billion in combined cost and revenue synergies."
context: "The split between cash tender and exchange is deliberate. The 2027 and 2028 notes mature too soon for an obligor substitution to make sense. Cash retirement is clean. The thirteen longer-dated series have enough remaining life to make the exchange rational for both sides. Holders exchanging into new PSKY notes should register what changes: the obligor moves from a WBD subsidiary to Paramount Skydance, the security shifts from unsecured to second-lien, and the credit exposure is now to a combined entity carrying substantially more leverage. The coupon is preserved. The credit profile is not the same. Two details require attention from custody teams. Exchange offer eligibility is restricted to QIBs and Regulation S holders; non-QIB domestic holders who miss this screen are ineligible regardless of consent delivery. Settlement does not occur at the June 17 expiration; it occurs on or shortly after acquisition close, leaving tendered positions in an unsettled state for a window that may extend several months depending on regulatory timelines."
