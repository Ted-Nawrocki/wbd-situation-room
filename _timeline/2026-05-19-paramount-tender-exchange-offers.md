title: "Paramount Launches $2.4 Billion Cash Tender Offers and $12.8 Billion Exchange Offers on WBD Notes"
fact: "On May 19, 2026, Paramount Skydance Corporation announced the commencement of concurrent cash tender offers and exchange offers targeting notes issued by Discovery Global Holdings, Inc. and Discovery Communications, LLC, the two WBD subsidiary issuers. The tender offers cover certain series of notes and are capped at up to $2.4 billion in aggregate principal amount. Holders whose notes are accepted in the tender offers will receive cash consideration, with accepted notes cancelled and retired. The exchange offers cover 13 series of notes totaling approximately $14.2 billion in aggregate principal amount, capped at $12.8 billion. Holders who validly tender their existing notes in the exchange offers will receive newly issued Paramount Skydance notes with matching currency, maturity dates, interest payment dates, and interest rates as the existing notes, with certain exceptions as specified in the offering memorandum. The offers expire at 5:00 p.m. New York time on June 17, 2026. Settlement for both the tender and exchange offers is expected to occur on or shortly after the closing date of the acquisition, anticipated in Q3 2026. Both the tender offers and exchange offers are conditioned on receipt of requisite noteholder consents in the concurrent WBD consent solicitations and on consummation of the acquisition. Participation in the Paramount offers requires holders to first deliver consents in the concurrent WBD consent solicitations. Paramount intends to fund tender consideration and accrued interest using cash on hand. Separately, Paramount disclosed plans to refinance its $49 billion acquisition bridge loan with approximately $39.5 billion of first-lien secured debt and $12.4 billion of second-lien secured debt, subject to market conditions. Paramount has committed to ratings agencies to delever to below 3.75x net debt to adjusted EBITDA by 2028 and 3.0x by 2029. Paramount also disclosed to eligible holders a target of over $6 billion in cost and revenue synergies from the combined platform."
context: "The $2.4 billion tender and $12.8 billion exchange are not the same transaction dressed differently. They represent two distinct debt management paths with fundamentally different outcomes for note holders.\n\nIn the tender offers, existing WBD notes are purchased for cash and retired. The WBD issuer entities cease to be obligors on those instruments. From a credit exposure standpoint, holders who tender into the cash offer are exiting the WBD capital structure entirely and receiving par value in cash.\n\nIn the exchange offers, holders surrender existing WBD notes and receive newly issued Paramount notes with matching terms. The issuer changes from Discovery Global Holdings or Discovery Communications to Paramount Skydance Corporation. The instrument itself -- maturity, coupon, currency -- is replicated, but the obligor is now the acquirer, not the target subsidiary. This is a meaningful distinction from a credit analysis perspective: holders are being asked to exchange exposure to a WBD subsidiary for exposure to a combined Paramount-WBD entity with considerably more leverage.\n\nThe conditionality structure is the most operationally significant feature of this entire exercise. The Paramount offers cannot settle unless: (1) the WBD consent solicitations achieve the required majority consents, and (2) the acquisition itself closes. That means holders face a scenario in which they deliver consents and tender or exchange their notes, then wait for an acquisition closing that is itself subject to regulatory approval. The settlement date is not a fixed calendar date -- it tracks the acquisition closing, which is targeted for Q3 2026 but subject to DOJ and European regulatory timelines.\n\nFor custody operations, this creates a prolonged position management challenge. Tendered notes are not immediately cancelled. They remain outstanding obligations of the WBD issuers until closing. Systems need to track the tendered status, the consent delivered status, and the pending settlement simultaneously, across multiple CUSIP and ISIN combinations, for what could be a multi-month window between the June 17 offer expiration and the Q3 closing.\n\nThe disclosed synergy target of over $6 billion and the deleveraging commitments to ratings agencies signal that Paramount is managing two audiences simultaneously: noteholders who need confidence in debt serviceability, and ratings agencies whose assessments will influence the cost of the permanent financing that replaces the bridge loan."
