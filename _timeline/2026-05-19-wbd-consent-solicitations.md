title: "WBD Subsidiaries Launch Consent Solicitations Across 17 Series of Notes"
fact: "On May 19, 2026, Warner Bros. Discovery announced that two of its wholly-owned subsidiaries -- Discovery Global Holdings, Inc. (formerly WarnerMedia Holdings, Inc.) and Discovery Communications, LLC -- commenced consent solicitations from holders of 17 series of outstanding notes. The notes carry interest rates ranging from 3.950% to 5.300% and mature between 2027 and 2052. Holders are asked to approve amendments to the indentures governing the notes. The primary proposed amendment would extend the deadline by which the WBD issuers are obligated to commence an offer for Junior Lien Exchange Notes from December 30, 2026 to the Merger Agreement end date of March 4, 2027, subject to extension. Additional amendments specify the terms on which any such Junior Lien Exchange Notes would be issued depending on whether the acquisition closes. Eligible holders who validly deliver consents by 5:00 p.m. New York time on May 26, 2026 and whose consents are accepted will receive a consent payment of $2.50 per $1,000 principal amount (or EUR 2.50 per EUR 1,000 for euro-denominated series). Payment is expected on or about May 29, 2026. Consent payments are funded by Paramount Skydance. The required consent threshold is a majority in aggregate principal amount of debt securities outstanding under each indenture. BofA Securities and Citigroup are acting as solicitation agents. Global Bondholder Services Corporation is acting as tabulation and information agent. The consent solicitations run concurrently with, but are separate from, Paramount's concurrent tender and exchange offers on certain of the same notes."
context: "Consent solicitations in acquisition contexts are rarely glamorous, but they are often determinative. This one has a clear mechanical purpose: the existing WBD indentures require the company to launch a Junior Lien Exchange offer by December 30, 2026. If the Paramount acquisition closes on the Q3 2026 target timeline, that deadline becomes irrelevant -- the combined company's capital structure will be governed by different instruments. But if the deal encounters delays, the December 30 deadline becomes a problem. WBD and Paramount need to extend that deadline to March 4, 2027 -- the Merger Agreement end date -- to preserve operational flexibility regardless of how the closing timeline moves.\n\nThe $2.50 per $1,000 consent fee is standard. It is an incentive payment, not compensation for economic concession. The amendments requested here do not change economic terms -- interest rates, maturities, and principal amounts are all unchanged. Holders are being asked to approve a scheduling adjustment in exchange for a de minimis cash payment funded by Paramount. The threshold -- majority in aggregate principal per indenture -- is achievable if large institutional holders are aligned, which they typically are when deal closure momentum is clear.\n\nFor operations teams, three things demand simultaneous attention. First, the consent deadline is May 26, 2026 -- five days from launch. That is a short window requiring rapid internal escalation for any holder that needs to review and instruct. Second, the consent solicitation runs concurrently with Paramount's separate tender and exchange offers on certain of the same notes, but participation in the Paramount offers requires that holders first deliver consents in these WBD solicitations. That sequencing dependency means a holder who misses the consent deadline effectively gates themselves out of the Paramount offers as well. Third, the payment timing -- expected May 29, 2026 -- creates a settlement event that custody systems need to track independently from the acquisition closing itself."
