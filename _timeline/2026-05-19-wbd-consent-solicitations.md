title: "WBD Subsidiaries Launch Consent Solicitations Across 17 Series of Notes"
fact: "On May 19, 2026, Warner Bros. Discovery announced that two wholly-owned subsidiaries -- Discovery Global Holdings, Inc. (formerly WarnerMedia Holdings, Inc.) and Discovery Communications, LLC -- commenced consent solicitations from holders of 17 series of outstanding notes. The notes carry interest rates from 3.950% to 5.300% and mature between 2027 and 2052. The primary proposed amendment extends the deadline by which the WBD issuers are obligated to commence an offer for Junior Lien Exchange Notes from December 30, 2026 to the Merger Agreement end date of March 4, 2027, subject to extension. Additional amendments specify terms for any such Junior Lien Exchange Notes depending on whether the acquisition closes. Eligible holders who deliver consents by 5:00 p.m. New York time on May 26, 2026 and whose consents are accepted will receive a consent payment of $2.50 per $1,000 principal amount (EUR 2.50 per EUR 1,000 for euro-denominated series), funded by Paramount. Payment is expected on or about May 29, 2026. The required threshold is a majority in aggregate principal amount per indenture. BofA Securities and Citigroup are solicitation agents. Global Bondholder Services Corporation is tabulation and information agent. Participation in Paramount's concurrent tender and exchange offers requires prior delivery of consents in these solicitations."
context: "The amendment is a scheduling adjustment, not an economic concession. The existing indentures require WBD to launch a Junior Lien Exchange offer by December 30, 2026. If the acquisition closes on the Q3 target, that deadline becomes irrelevant. If closing slips, it becomes a live obligation at an awkward moment. Extending it to the Merger Agreement end date removes that risk. The five-day consent window is short. Missing the May 26 deadline gates a holder out of both Paramount offers for that series -- an irreversible consequence of a missed processing deadline."
