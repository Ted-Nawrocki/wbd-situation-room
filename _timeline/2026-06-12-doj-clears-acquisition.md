title: "DOJ Antitrust Division Clears Paramount Acquisition of WBD Without Conditions"
fact: "On June 12, 2026, multiple outlets reported that the U.S. Department of Justice Antitrust Division cleared Paramount Skydance's acquisition of Warner Bros. Discovery without requiring divestitures, behavioral remedies, or other concessions. Politico first reported the clearance, with Variety and NBC News corroborating, each citing people familiar with the matter. DOJ officials concluded the transaction did not pose a threat to competition and declined to challenge it. Paramount issued a statement thanking the Department for its review and noting that other agencies had also completed reviews and provided clearance to date. As of the initial reporting, no formal DOJ press release had been published, and a confirmed closing timeline had not been disclosed. The California Attorney General's office, joined by the New York Attorney General's office, continues an active review of the transaction. PSKY shares rose approximately 3.8% and WBD shares approximately 0.4% in Friday post-close trading."
context: "DOJ clearance was the largest remaining gating item, and unconditional clearance is the cleanest version of that outcome. No divestitures means the asset perimeter Paramount agreed to buy is the asset perimeter it gets, which keeps the disclosed synergy math intact. The open risk shifts to the state attorneys general. California and New York continuing their review does not stop a closing on its own, but a coordinated state action could. For the debt offers, this clearance moves the conditional settlement closer to reality. The offers settle on closing, and closing now has its principal federal hurdle behind it. The reporting is sourced rather than confirmed by an official DOJ release, so the precise terms are worth verifying against the Department's own statement once it posts."
